> 来源: [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.16177v1)

作者: Matteo Tomasetto, Nicolò Botteghi, Gabriele Bruni 等

Reinforcement learning (RL) has recently emerged as a promising feedback control strategy for nonlinear and complex dynamical systems. However, RL algorithms are sample inefficient and require a large number of interaction with the environment to synthesize optimal control strategies. Consequently, applications of RL are typically limited to sparse sensors and actuators due to the curse of dimensionality entailed by the exploration-exploitation dilemma in high-dimensional spaces.
