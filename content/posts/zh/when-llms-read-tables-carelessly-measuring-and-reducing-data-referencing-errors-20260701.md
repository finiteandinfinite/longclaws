> 来源: [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.32029v1)

作者: Yuqing Yang, Qi Zhu, Zhen Han 等

While large language models (LLMs) perform well on table tasks, they still make data referencing errors (DREs), i.e., incorrectly citing or omitting table values, despite understanding the table structure. Beyond final-answer accuracy, DREs directly compromise the correctness and reliability of intermediate reasoning steps. Yet prior studies have only offered limited, small-scale analyses.
