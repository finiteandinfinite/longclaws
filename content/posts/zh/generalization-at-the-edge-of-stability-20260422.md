# Generalization at the Edge of Stability

> 来源: [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19740v1)

作者: Mario Tuci, Caner Korkmaz, Umut Şimşekli 等

Training modern neural networks often relies on large learning rates, operating at the edge of stability, where the optimization dynamics exhibit oscillatory and chaotic behavior. Empirically, this regime often yields improved generalization performance, yet the underlying mechanism remains poorly understood. In this work, we represent stochastic optimizers as random dynamical systems, which often converge to a fractal attractor set (rather than a point) with a smaller intrinsic dimension. Build
