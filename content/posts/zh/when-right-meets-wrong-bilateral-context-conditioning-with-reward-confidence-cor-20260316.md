# When Right Meets Wrong: Bilateral Context Conditioning with Reward-Confidence Correction for GRPO

> 来源: [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.13134v1)

作者: Yu Li, Tian Lan, Zhengling Qi

Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) has emerged as an effective method for training reasoning models. While it computes advantages based on group mean, GRPO treats each output as an independent sample during the optimization and overlooks a vital structural signal: the natural contrast between correct and incorrect solutions within the same group, thus ignoring the rich, comparative data that could be leveraged by explicitly pitting successful reasoning traces against failed ones.
